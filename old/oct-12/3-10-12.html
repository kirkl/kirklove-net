Stop Comparing. Instead Take Stock.

You get in trouble when compare yourself to others you think have more than you. Oh, look they have more money. He's in better shape. She's more popular. They go out more. It's a no-win situation once you start.

Conversely, you can think you are better than others, too, which is equally dangerous, if not worse.

Not comparing is hard, especially here in New York where its the name of the game and zip codes define. Instead I'm focusing on taking stock of what I have. And I have so much. An amazing wife. A beautiful daughter. A lovable dog. Incredible family and friends. My health. A wonderful home. Once I run down what I have, comparing it serves no purpose. I just enjoy what I have.
