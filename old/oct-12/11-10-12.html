Hire great writers

Jason Fried, co-founder of 37Signals is someone I admire. The three words in the healine above are his best advice ever. I keep a scan (pictured below) of the page from ReWork that explains the thinking behind "Hire great writers". It's no coincidence it's written wonderfully. The last line is particularly perfct: Writing is today's currency for good ideas.

What a powerful line. As I file though my mind the people I admire most are all great writers and communicators.

As for my own writing, I'm decent. Maybe even above average. On a scale of 1 to 10, I'd be a solid six. On some days I may even be a seven. My goal is to be a consistent nine. I've got work to do.

The good news is anyone can get better at writing. The steps aren't a secret:

  • Read other great writing and learn from it.
  • Develop a voice. Be yourself. And be genuine.
  • Write a lot and often. You'll get better.
  • Avoid the "Curse of Knowledge". No one is inside your head.
  • Edit. Then edit again. Then go back and edit yet again.
  • Get outside feedback when possible.
  • Ruthlessly trim (like editing, but harsher). Less is Less.
  • Repeat all these items over and over.
