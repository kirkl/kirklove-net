Bitching

Yesterday I wrote about a great loss in my life. A loss an unceremonious select few experience. That kind of loss gives you perspective and makes you super appreciative of what you have. I could pretend I always had that positive spin on things, but that would be a lie. It's taken me years to regain a positive attitude and during that time I was known to bitch a lot. Let's just say I had my fair share of pity parties, too. There are still days when the "bitcher" in me wins, but I'm happy to say they are less and less.

What I've learned is bitching gets you nothing except more upset. Plus, no one cares you are bitching. You are just hurting yourself.

Instead what I do now is focus on the following list. I repeat them like a mantra:

  • Focus on what I have, not what I don't
  • Eschew materialistic things
  • Don't try to control others, accept them
  • Let things go
  • Choose to be happy
  • Put others ahead of yourself

The remarkable thing is this really works. It seems like mumbo-jumbo, but it's not. If you find yourself in a bitchy mood take five minutes to reflect on why, then run down this list and really try to apply them. I guarantee you'll stop bitching and you'll feel a ton better.
