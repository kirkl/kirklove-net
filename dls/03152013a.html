> 50

Sometimes the inflow of online data overwhelms me. It actually affects me physically. I don't like it. To combat that I've greatly reduced my interaction on social sites (and happen to be building one I think strikes the right balance). One tip I've found works really well for me is to significantly reduce the number of people I follow. To that avail I've put a hard cap on my following of less than 50 people per platform. Yes, no matter the social platform, I follow less than 50 people. It's tough, because I don't want to piss off anyone (silly really when you think about that) and two, a lot of people post great stuff, but I only have so much mental capacity. However, the pros have greatly outweighed the cons.

The >50 limit has led to several serious improvements:

  1. There is a lot less "noise". A ton less actually.
  2. No mindlessly scrolling through stuff (again less noise)
  3. Check platforms far less (which I see as a good thing)
  4. Much greater appreciation for what I do see
  5. Formed stronger bonds with the people I do follow
  6. Don't miss what I don't see
  7. Reduces the need to "compete" or "compare"
  8. Frees me to have much stronger real-world interactions
  9. Calms my mind
  10. Forces me to re-evaluate who I am following and why

Maybe this all sounds silly and I shouldn't worry about it. But, it works for me. Balance is important to me. I love the online social space (a lot). I just don't like that my addictive personalty can sometimes get consumed and overwhelmed by it. Hence the cap.

2013 Mar 1550 is my cap
