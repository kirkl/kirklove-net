Humble Beginnings

If you asked me three months ago if I could build a website I would have said no. Granted I had some ideas and could fake my way through it, but I didn't "know how" it all worked exactly. It was mostly copying and pasting other code (not that there is anything wrong with that at all).

Fast forward to today and I would say, maybe. It's probably more like yes, but I like to under-promise and over-deliver. The point is I'm learning more and more each day. Pieces are clicking. I still only know a fraction and a very small fraction at that, but I have a base and I'm building on that.

What was once a mountain staring up in front of me, is still a mountain, but I've taken a few steps. I can look back and see the progress I've made and that's super encouraging. There is also enough distance between my start and where I want to be to not turn back now. The main reason though is still I really want to see what's at the summit (this is the part where I'm supposed to say what lies at the top of the mountain is whatever you bring up there with you).
